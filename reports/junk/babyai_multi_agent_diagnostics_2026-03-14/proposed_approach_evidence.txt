BabyAI 4-Agent Reviewer Topology: Proposed Approach Evidence
Date: 2026-03-14

Purpose
- Preserve the concrete metrics and trace examples needed to describe the 4-agent shared-policy topology in the paper's proposed approach section.

Topology Under Test
- Topology name: `planner_executor_reviewers`
- Shared policy across four roles:
  - `planner`
  - `planner_reviewer`
  - `executor`
  - `executor_reviewer`
- Intended message flow:
  1. Planner produces longer grounded reasoning.
  2. Planner reviewer approves, retries, or repairs planner output before the executor sees it.
  3. Executor emits BabyAI-native `Thought:` + `Action:`.
  4. Executor reviewer approves or retries the executor output before deterministic validation and `env.step()`.

Quantitative Interpretation
- The 4-agent reviewer topology peaked at `step 50`.
- The degradation from `step 50 -> 100 -> 150` is monotonic.
- The degradation is primarily executor-side:
  - planner fallback remained `0.0` at all evaluated checkpoints
  - planner invalid format remained `0.0` at all evaluated checkpoints
  - executor native format violations rose sharply from `0.0667 -> 0.3444 -> 0.5667`
- This supports the claim that reviewer gating kept planner outputs consumable, but did not prevent executor quality from collapsing over longer training.

Suggested Paper Framing
- Proposed architecture claim:
  - reviewer-gated multi-agent decomposition can keep planner outputs consumable even when raw planner drafts drift.
- Limitation supported by the data:
  - reviewer gating alone was insufficient to stabilize the executor over longer PPO training.
- Best empirical evidence:
  - strong early checkpoint at `step 50`
  - monotonic degradation at `step 100` and `step 150`
  - trace evidence showing planner repair succeeds while late executor outputs still collapse into invalid format.
